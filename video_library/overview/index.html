The Media Library - Overview

The LSVT Media Library is a tool that will automatically upload and encode your video files for you. Once you put a video in your Media Library, you can use, and reuse it as many times as you like.

Note

The video you upload is commonly referred to as your “source” video.

When you add a new video to the Media Library, you can do this by going right to your Media Library in the Super User Dashboard, or you can also do it right inline, while you are adding or editing a Course.

Each video you add will need a “Video Name” and a “Video Description.” These are for your internal reference only and are not displayed to your user on the front end. This is simply to make it easy for you to find and use these videos at a future time. We will put a “Last Modified” stamp on each video in your Media Library as well to assist you for future reference.

Tip

Once you have added video to your Media Library, you can swap out the actual video at a future time, however, this in itself will not add/replace this new video to any Chapters or areas that have used the prior video. You will still need to navigate to that desired chapter and update it to use the newer video from this Media Library entry. This is a fail-safe to ensure you don’t add/update anything unintentionally.

Encoding Settings

When encoding videos, you will want to have as high quality video as you can to start with - the ole’ Garbage In = Garbage Out analogy. Below are some answers to commonly asked questions about this process to best assist you in making your video look the best it can.

QUESTION:
What video format should my source video be?

ANSWER:
Pretty much anything that is “really” a video. CLICK HERE to jump down this page and take a detailed look at acceptable video formats.

QUESTION:
What resolution or size should my video be?

ANSWER:
We playback videos at a 16:9 ratio, ideally 1280 (width) by 720 (height). However any size of 16:9 will scale in fine. If your video is not a 16:9 ratio, we will help you and add a “pad” around it to fill up the space to that ratio. We don’t goof up your video by stretching it to an odd size that looks bad.

QUESTION:
How long will it take to encode my video?

ANSWER:
That depends how fast your Internet upload speed is to upload it, and then also how long (in duration) your video is to encode it.

The basic process is:

  1. We will upload your video, and you will see a progress bar on this process. A five minute video typically takes less than 30 seconds to upload.
  2. We will encode your video to all of the different target formats to be compatible across desktop and mobile formats. A five minute video typically takes about three minutes to encode.

Tip

When you are adding video to the Media Library, a small window will pop open to indicate the process of that one video uploading and encoding. You can navigate your main window back to the Media Library and upload a second (or more) videos in additional pop up windows. When they are done uploading and encoding they will save back to the Media Library on their own for convenience.

Encoding Specifics

Once you upload your source video, we will encode it to these different formats for adaptive streaming, and we will “Auto” select the right one for the user, based on their available bandwidth and/or device, dynamically during playback. Additionally, advanced users may use the “gear icon” ( ) on the video player to indicate their preferred format, and override the “Auto” setting.

The width and height measurements noted below are used as the maximum values for the resize, preserving aspect ratio. So if you wanted to resize a 300x400 video into 100x100, the resulting video would be 75x100 pixels.

Note

When you are uploading your video, we ask you “Is Your Video High Definition?” And if you select “Yes” then we will utilize the top two HD encoding settings, as well as all of the others. If you select “No” we will simply not use those top two HD encoding settings.

Width Height Bitrate Name
1280 720 1500k 720p HD
854 480 800k 480p High
640 360 400k 360p Med
432 240 200k 240p Low

What format of videos can you upload?

The Media Library can accept any format of video that you upload, that is indeed a video (a Powerpoint file is not a video, no matter how many “cool” slide transitions you know how to make - nor is an Animated GIF... sorry).

Common acceptable formats you can upload are:

Suffix Type Notes
.MP4 MPEG-4 Most common format right now
.MOV QuickTime File format Created commonly by Apple computers
.MPG MPEG-1 and MPEG-2 Older format, but still widely used. Also may be known as .MPEG, .MP2, .MPE, etc.
.M4V MPEG-4 File format for videos for iPods and PlayStation Portables developed by Apple
.FLV Flash Video Use of the H.264 and AAC compression formats in the FLV file format has some limitations and authors of Flash Player strongly encourage everyone to embrace the new standard F4V file format.
.F4V Replacement for FLV Basically an MPEG-4
.WMV Windows Media Video Created commonly by computers running Windows
.rm RealMedia Made for RealPlayer
